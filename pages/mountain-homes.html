Mountain Building Expertise

Turning mountain challenges into design opportunities

Steep Terrain

Challenge: Difficult access and sloped building sites
Our Solution: Multi-level designs that follow natural contours, creating dramatic spaces with walk-out levels and terraced outdoor areas

Weather Extremes

Challenge: Snow loads, ice, wind, and temperature swings
Our Solution: Engineered roof systems, proper insulation, weather-resistant materials, and strategic design to handle mountain weather

Utility Challenges

Challenge: Limited access to utilities and services
Our Solution: Well and septic systems, propane solutions, backup generators, and satellite internet coordination

Environmental Sensitivity

Challenge: Protecting natural beauty and ecosystems
Our Solution: Minimal site disturbance, native landscaping, sustainable materials, and designs that preserve views and wildlife corridors

Mountain Home FAQs

How long is the building season in the mountains?

We build year-round in Cashiers, though winter weather can occasionally slow progress. Our experienced teams know how to work safely and efficiently in mountain conditions.

What utilities are available for mountain lots?

Utility availability varies by location. We assess each site for electric, well water, septic systems, propane, and internet options during our initial consultation.

Do mountain homes require special permits?

Yes, mountain construction often requires additional permits for septic, wells, and driveways. We handle all permit applications and regulatory approvals.

How do you handle steep lots?

Steep terrain creates opportunities for dramatic multi-level designs, walk-out basements, and enhanced views. We specialize in making challenging sites into stunning homes.

What about rental management for investment properties?

We can recommend established rental management companies in the Cashiers area and design features that appeal to vacation rental guests.

Are mountain homes more expensive to build?

Mountain construction can involve additional costs for site preparation, utilities, and access, but our experience helps minimize surprises and maximize value.
